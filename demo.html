  1. In the Mood for Love

    2.   Set in 1960s Hong Kong, this film uses delicate emotional expressions, unique visual style and subtle Eastern aesthetics to depict a love affair outside marriage that is filled with repression and missed opportunities.

  2. Moonlight

    3.   The evaluation of The New York Times states that this film transcends the label of "political correctness" and is a universal poem about growth, loneliness and self-identity.

  3. No Country for Old Men

    4.   Through the chain of assassinations triggered by hunter Moss' embezzlement of drug dealers' money, this story exposes the disintegration of order caused by violence and the nihilism of good and evil in human nature.

  4. Enternal Sunshine for the Spotless Mind

    5.   Through the memory-erasing technology of "Emotional Discharge Clinic", the symbiotic relationship between pain and sweetness in love is dismantled, and the question is raised: "If the scars could be erased, would you be willing to give up all of love?"

  5. Get Out

    6.   A black photographer visits the family of his white girlfriend and gets caught in the "horror under the sun" - an identity transplant conspiracy disguised as a cross-racial relationship.
